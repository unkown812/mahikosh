Home Energy Efficiency: Save Money and Reduce Carbon Emissions

Published July 2, 2026 · 7 min read

Home energy use accounts for a significant portion of personal carbon emissions. Improving your home's energy efficiency not only reduces your environmental impact but also lowers your monthly bills. This guide covers practical steps for Indian households.

Switch to 5-Star BEE Rated Appliances

The Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) star rating system helps Indian consumers choose energy-efficient appliances. A 5-star AC uses approximately 30% less electricity than a 3-star model. When replacing appliances, always check the BEE label.

Optimize Air Conditioner Usage

Set your AC to 24°C instead of 18°C — this reduces electricity consumption by up to 30%. Clean filters monthly, use ceiling fans to circulate cool air, and seal windows to prevent cool air from escaping.

Switch to LED Lighting

LED bulbs use 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs and last 25x longer. Replacing all bulbs in a typical Indian home can save approximately ₹5,000-8,000 per year on electricity bills.

Eliminate Phantom Power

Electronics in standby mode — TVs, chargers, computers, gaming consoles — consume 5-10% of household electricity. Use power strips with switches to completely cut power when devices aren't in use.

Use Solar Water Heaters

India receives abundant sunshine year-round. Solar water heaters can reduce your electricity bill by 15-20% and save up to 1.5 tonnes of CO₂ per year. Government subsidies are available in most states.
