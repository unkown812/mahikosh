Why Meat Has Higher Emissions

Animal agriculture requires significantly more resources than plant farming. Livestock produces methane, feed cultivation requires land and water, and transportation and refrigeration add to the carbon cost. Red meat (especially beef) has the highest footprint, while poultry and fish are lower but still above most plant proteins.

How to Reduce Your Food Carbon Footprint

  • Try plant-based proteins: Lentils, chickpeas, and tofu have a fraction of the carbon footprint of meat
  • Eat seasonal and local: Reduces transport and storage emissions by up to 10x
  • Reduce food waste: Plan meals and use leftovers to avoid unnecessary emissions
