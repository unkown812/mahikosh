Eco-Friendly Transportation: A Guide to Green Commuting

Published July 2, 2026 · 6 min read

Transportation is the fastest-growing source of greenhouse gas emissions worldwide. Choosing eco-friendly commuting options is one of the most impactful changes you can make. Here's your complete guide to green transportation.

Walking & Cycling (Zero Emissions)

Best for trips under 5 km. Zero CO₂ emissions, improves cardiovascular health, and costs nothing. In EcoTrack, walking or biking earns 10x more EcoBucks per km than driving. Most Indian cities have 40-60% of trips under 5 km — a huge opportunity for emission-free travel.

Public Transit (Bus & Train)

Buses produce 0.08 kg CO₂/km and trains produce 0.04 kg CO₂/km — 53-76% less than cars. India's expanding metro networks and bus rapid transit systems make public transit increasingly convenient. Switch 2 days per week to cut commute emissions by 40%.

Electric Vehicles

EVs produce zero tailpipe emissions and, when charged from India's grid, have 30-50% lower lifecycle emissions than petrol cars. Government FAME-II subsidies make EVs increasingly affordable. Note that walking, biking, and transit remain lower-impact than even EVs.

Carpooling & Ride-Sharing

Sharing a car with even one other person cuts per-person emissions in half. Apps like BlaBlaCar make intercity carpooling easy. For daily commutes, coordinate with colleagues to share rides and reduce traffic congestion too.
